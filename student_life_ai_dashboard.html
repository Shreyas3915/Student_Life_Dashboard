Model architecture & feature engineering

This dashboard uses a multi-output weighted linear scoring model — interpretable, fast, and tuned on domain knowledge. Each of the 5 targets has an independently calibrated formula.

Target variable formulas
Feature weights used to compute each score (clamped 0–100)
Burnout risk formula
Higher = more burnout
  • Stress × 7
  • Parental pressure × 4
  • (10 – Sleep) × 3
  • Backlogs × 4
  • (100 – Attendance) × 0.3
  • Screen time × 1.5
  • (10 – Motivation) × 4
  • Hostel flag × 3
  • – Exercise × 2
  • – Friends × 1.5
  • – Study hrs × 1
Placement score formula
Higher = better placement chances
  • CGPA × 6
  • Coding skills × 3
  • Internships × 8
  • Projects × 3
  • Certifications × 2
  • Attendance × 0.2
  • Motivation × 1.5
  • Study hrs × 1
  • – Backlogs × 2
Backlog risk formula
Higher = more likely to accumulate backlogs
  • Backlogs × 8
  • (100 – Attendance) × 0.4
  • (10 – CGPA) × 4
  • Stress × 3
  • (100 – Assignments) × 0.3
  • (8 – Sleep) × 2
  • – Study hrs × 2
  • – Motivation × 2
Loneliness score formula
Higher = more social isolation
  • (10 – Friends) × 6
  • Hostel flag × 4
  • Stress × 2
  • Screen time × 1.5
  • (8 – Sleep) × 2
  • – Club participation × 4
  • – Exercise × 2
Productivity trend formula
Higher = more productive student week-over-week

Score = (Study hrs × 5) + (Motivation × 4) + (Attendance × 0.3) – (Stress × 2) – (Screen time × 1.5) + (Exercise × 2) + (CGPA × 2)
Weekly trend is simulated as a ±4% random walk around the base score to model real-world variation.

Dataset generation methodology
How the synthetic records were created

The 20 student profiles were hand-crafted to represent a realistic distribution across academic performance tiers (high achievers, mid-range, at-risk), living situations (hostel vs day scholars), and social profiles. Features were assigned to create meaningful correlations — e.g. high stress & low sleep students consistently score higher on burnout while lower on placement. Target variables are then computed deterministically from the feature formulas above, ensuring full reproducibility and explainability without any black-box components.
